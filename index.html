INFO PAGE: Nutrition & History

The Sunchoke (*Helianthus tuberosus*) is native to North America. Unlike potatoes, its primary carbohydrate is **Inulin**, a prebiotic fiber, not starch. This is fantastic for gut flora but can cause digestive issues in some if consumed in large quantities.

MINDFULNESS & SUSTAINABILITY

The process of growing, harvesting, and preparing Sunchokes lends itself naturally to mindfulness and sustainable living. The plant is incredibly resilient and produces high yields, requiring less intervention than most crops—a model of sustainability.

Mindfulness Tip

When harvesting sunchokes in late fall or winter, take time to appreciate the tuber's intricate, irregular shape. Focus on the grounding feeling of pulling food directly from the earth, fostering a mindful connection to your food source.

Sustainability Practice

Sunchokes are a highly **sustainable** choice because they are perennial and grow vigorously in poor soil. They also require very little water compared to conventional root vegetables. The leftover stalks can be composted, creating a perfect zero-waste cycle.
